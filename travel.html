Travel & Accommodations

Getting There

The venue is up a dirt road 6km north of downtown Hope on the west side of Trans-Can 1. There will be a shuttle from the venue to hotels in Hope. See the "Venue" page for more details on the venue.

By Air:

The closest major airport is Vancouver (YVR). There is a bus from Pacific Terminal that stops in Hope, or you can rent a car.

On the Ground:

From Vancouver: Take Trans-Can 1 EAST to Hope. Tah dah!

From East/North: Take Trans-Can 1 WEST to Kamloops, then stay with BC 5 ("The Coquihalla") heading SOUTH. Turn off onto Trans-Can 1 in Hope.

On Public Transit: There is a train station in Hope serviced by Via Rail, but its service is spotty (it's a flag-stop). There are a few different buses between Vancouver and Calgary or Kamloops that stop in Hope (e.g. Rider Express).

If you're coming from the US by car... it really depends on where you're coming from. Google Maps it.

Local Accommodations

Hotels:

Hope is a very small town, so its hotels are more on the "quaint" side rather than luxury or chains (note: the "Red Roof Inn" is not the famous American chain). But it does have a lot of them.

If chain-hotels are desired, Chilliwack has many such options about 30 minutes west on Trans-Can 1. The Holiday Inn Express is particularly convenient.

Camping/RV:

Mid-October is chilly, but camping in this area is always a good time in the autumn. Hope has a large number of tent and RV campgrounds, as does E. C. Manning Provincial Park to the East and Skagit Valley Provincial Park to the south.
